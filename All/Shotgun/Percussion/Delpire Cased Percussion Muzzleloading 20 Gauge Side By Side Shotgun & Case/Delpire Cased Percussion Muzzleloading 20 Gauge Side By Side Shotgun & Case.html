Description French made percussion muzzleloading shotgun marked "Delpire". Manufactured circa 1850 in Paris, France. 27-3/4" twist steel Belgian proofed 20 gauge barrels with percussion hook breech, concave smooth rib with "Delpire ARQer DU ROI A PARIS", followed by an image of golden wheat sheaf, all in gold. Also has single bead front sight. Back action floral engraved locks marked "Delpire A PARIS" with floral engraved hammers. Control by double triggers. Birdseye grain walnut checkered stock with floral engraved hardware. Gun is cased in green felt-lined oak trunk case with leather shot flask, copper James Dixon powder flask, ebony handled screw driver, two piece wooden cleaning rod with brushes and swabs, wad cutter and ball mold. CONDITION: 90% fading twist pattern on barrels turning to a brown patina. Locks and hardware have been polished and are fading back to a grey patina. Wood has an added coat of linseed oil with checkering worn flat, showing some usage marks and dings. Dark bores with light pitting and crisp mechanics. Case and accoutrements are in good condition with case showing storage marks and wear. The case is missing keys. CALIBER: 20 Gauge. FFL STATUS: Antique. S/N: NSN.
