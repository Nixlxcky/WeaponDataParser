Description Manufactured circa 1924 in Germany. Right side of the barrel is marked "EMIL KERNER & SOHN WITTENER". No visible import marks. Serial number on the barrel with no serial number on the forearm. "JRD2" hand etched on the underside of the barrel. O/U 30" barrel with solid, matted rib. Front sight is absent. Case colored frame with a fine game scene engraving of a dog chasing a wood cock on the left with fox/quail on the right. Double triggers and tang safety. Oiled wood stock and forearm. Fine cut checkering on the wrist and forearm. White inlays on each side of the wrist, top of the comb and bottom of the round knob pistol grip. European cheek piece and small width sling swivels. Metal bullet trap with engraved face (rifle trap, meaning this gun likely had a rifle barrel set at some point). Carved horn trigger guard. Added Pachmayr brown rubber butt pad. Bores appear to be choked at full/full. LOP: 14" to the end of the butt and 13-1/8" to the end of the wood. CONDITION: Metal is very good overall with the barrels having about 70% finish. Frame is very fine. Wood has light scratches and scrapes. Bore are bright. Mechanically fine. CALIBER: 12 Gauge. FFL STATUS: Curio & Relic. S/N: 2306.
