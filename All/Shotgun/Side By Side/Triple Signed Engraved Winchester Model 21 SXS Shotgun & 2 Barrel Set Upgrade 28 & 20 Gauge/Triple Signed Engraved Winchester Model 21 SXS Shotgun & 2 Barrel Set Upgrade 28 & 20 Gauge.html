Description Stunning upgrade of a Winchester Model 21 manufactured in 1937 as a Standard Grade by Winchester Repeating Arms Company, as confirmed by The Cody Firearms Records Office. Upgraded by an unknown professional gunsmith and engraved by three master Liege gunsmiths: Angelo Bee, Rocco Capece and Jean Diet. Gun features two sets of Winchester manufactured SXS barrels. One barrel in 20 gauge with blued finish, 2 -3/4" chambers, matted rib, twin bead sights, 27 -3/4" length and choked Full and Modified with ejectors. The other barrel in 28 gauge with blued finish, 2-3/4" chambers, matted rib, twin bead sights, 28" length and choked Full and Full with ejectors. Modified blued Model 21 action with sideplates with left hand side embellished with floral gold design and four gold quail, signed by master engraver "R. Capece". Right hand side is decorated with floral gold border design and four gold doves in flight, signed by master engraver "J H Diet". Floorplate and trigger guard are engraved with gold border design with three gold ducks in flight and a gold quail on the trigger bow and signed by master engraver "A. Bee" right of the gold plated Winchester single selective trigger. Gun has European figured walnut stock with English style grip, wrist carving and checkering and checkered wood butt. Each set of barrels is fitted with its own checkered and carved beavertail forearm with gold decorated roller style latch. LOP: 15-3/8". DAC: 1-1/2". DAH: 1-5/8". Weight: 7.0 lbs. with 20 gauge barrels and 8.0 lbs. with 28 gauge barrels. This fine gun is cased in a fitted leather and oak case with nickel plated oil bottle and snap caps with Winchester Maker's label. Includes a research letter from the Cody Firearms Records Office. CONDITION: 98% barrel blue with light edge and assembly wear. Action retains 98% finish with sharp engraving. 95% original wood finish with some visible usage dings on butt and forearm carry areas.
