Description The Cody Firearms Records Office confirms this gun was manufactured in 1952 by Winchester Repeating Arms and originally shipped to Edwin K, Tryon Co., Philadelphia, PA. Later upgraded by Frank Pachmayr at unknown date for donation to Pachmayr supported organization. Blued 30" SXS Winchester proof steel barrels with full and full fixed chokes, ejectors, 3" chambers, and twin bone bead sights with matted rib marked "Custom By Pachmayr" in gold. Barrels embellished with deep relief floral engraving at muzzle and breech. Polished steel deep relief engraved receiver with side plates. Left side features two dogs watching three quails in flight, all in gold. Right side features two dogs watching two walking pheasants, all in gold. Floor plate has three gold ducks in various stages of flight signed by master engraver Reno Greco. Action is fitted with "Miller" non selective single trigger and non automatic safety with side clips and pierced opening lever. Action is marked "DUCK" with blued deep relief engraved trigger guard. Walnut feather crotch Fleur de Lis checkered pistol grip stock with Pachmayr "Old English" solid rubber recoil pad with grip cap inscribed "Donated by Nanitta and Frank Pachmayr". Matching Winchester style Fleur de Lis checkered beavertailed forearm with deep relief engraved roller latch and forearm iron. LOP: 14-1/2". DAC: 1-1/2". DAH: 2". Weight: 8lbs. This gun has incredible eye appeal and presents as a special work of art. Includes a research letter from the Cody Firearms Records Office. CONDITION: 98% overall with light handling marks, sharp engraving and sharp checkering. Excellent bores and mechanics. CALIBER: 12 Gauge. FFL STATUS: Curio & Relic. S/N: 18839.
