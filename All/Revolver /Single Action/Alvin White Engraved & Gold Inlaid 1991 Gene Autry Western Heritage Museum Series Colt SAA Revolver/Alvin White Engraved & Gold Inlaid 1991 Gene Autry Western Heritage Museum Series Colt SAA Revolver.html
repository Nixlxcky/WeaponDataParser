Description Third generation Colt SAA revolver, manufactured circa 1979. Colt records show it was shipped to Outdoor Sports Headquarters in Illinois. Eventually acquired by the Gene Autry Western Heritage Museum, it was auctioned at their fundraising gala in 1991. The museum commissioned master engraver Alvin White to engrave and embellish this extraordinary SAA. It was the first of approximately ten revolvers to be offered at the annual fundraiser auctions over the next decade and can be seen in The Colt Engraving Book, Volume Two by R.L Wilson. Standard model markings on the left side of the barrel with the company address on top. Typical patent dates and rampant Colt. All markings, minus the "VP" proof, are in gold inlay. Barrel has a gold inlaid image of the museum building on the left side and "Annual Gala/Gene Autry Western Heritage Museum" etched on the right. Gold inlay "10 gallon" Stetson on the right cylinder in addition to a silver inlay cowboy and rampant horse.
